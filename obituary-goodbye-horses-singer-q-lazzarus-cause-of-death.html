    According to The Rolling Stone, a close friend of Q Lazzarus, Eva Aridjis, confirmed the singer-death.

    The 61-year-old singer-songwriter died after a brief illness, but no cause of death was given.

    Aridjis discussed Lazzarus’ vibrant and exuberant energy in an interview with The Rolling Stone: “Q possessed one of those life forces that, because it was so vital, radiant, and exuberant, you couldn’t imagine it being extinguished or ceasing to exist. Despite having had a difficult life, she was not cynical.”

    Q Lazzarus, real name Diane Luckey, was born in New Jersey and died at the age of 61. The viewing was also held in New Jersey, according to her obituary.

    She rose to fame after her song “Goodbye Horses” was featured in Jonathan Demme’s 1991 film “The Silence of the Lambs.”

    Her other songs were also featured in Demme’s films, and the song became a cult favorite. She, on the other hand, faded from view around the mid-1990s.

    Lazzarus’ death was only brought to public attention on August 19, despite the fact that she died a month earlier.

    Aridjis, on the other hand, revealed that Lazzarus was planning a comeback concert: “[…] She exuded energy, passion, and wit. She was also full of ideas. We were planning a “comeback concert” with some of her original bandmates at the time of her death.”
