    George Stephanopoulos has a net worth of $18 million, predominantly amassed through his career as a TV host and advisor for the US government over a span of more than 30 years.

    George Robert Stephanopoulos, born on February 10, 1961, in Fall River, Massachusetts, is an American author, political advisor, journalist, and broadcaster of Greek descent. He gained prominence during Bill Clinton’s presidential campaign, serving as the communications director and later as an advisor in strategy and policy for the Clinton government. His extensive involvement in politics and his role as a TV personality have significantly contributed to his substantial net worth.

    Raised with the expectation of becoming a lawyer like his father, a priest, Stephanopoulos pursued a Bachelor’s degree in political science at Columbia University and furthered his education with a Master of Arts degree in Theology from Balliol College, Oxford.

    His political career began with his work in Michael Dukakis’ 1988 presidential campaign, continuing with his involvement in Bill Clinton’s campaign, and subsequently serving as press secretary and later as the Senior Advisor for Policy and Strategic Planning during Clinton’s presidency. His resignation in 1996 marked the conclusion of his time at the White House.

    Stephanopoulos’ 1999 book, “All Too Human: A Political Education,” became a New York Times bestseller, shedding light on the challenges he encountered as the spokesperson for the Clinton administration. Following his stint at the White House, he transitioned to a role as a political reporter for ABC News’ “World News Tonight” and commenced co-hosting “Good Morning America” in 2009. From 2014 onwards, Stephanopoulos has been the chief anchor of ABC News, and also makes frequent appearances on GMA. His prominence within the political landscape and his role as a TV anchor have notably contributed to the growth of his net worth.

    In his personal life, Stephanopoulos married Alexandra Wentworth in 2001, who is a comedian, writer, and actress. The couple has two daughters and resides in Manhattan. Stephanopoulos has had minor run-ins with the law, including driving with an expired license plate and fleeing from the scene of an accident in 1995, although the latter case was dropped.
